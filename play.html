Using Technology to improve chess

I recently conducted a training session for chess trainers on how to use technology to improve chess play. You can find the PDF version here Let me summarize it below

If there is one site for learning, it has to be lichess.org You can learn tactics, openings, endgames with lichess study

Openings

  • Youtube channels
    • St Louis Chess Club
    • Hanging Pawns
    • ChessNetwork - not only openings
    • Any channel that you like
  • Chessable
  • Lichess Study

Middle Games

  • Most websites have chess tactics - lichess, chess.com
  • For middle game strategy, content is more difficult to find online. You can see YT channels I've mentioned earlier
  • For grandmaster games, try www.chessgames.com

EndGames

  • Most websites have chess tactics - lichess, chess.com
  • For middle game strategy, content is more difficult to find online. You can see YT channels I've mentioned earlier
  • For grandmaster games, try www.chessgames.com

Analysis of games using engines

  • Stockfish is best - you can find the engine online and use with SCID
  • lichess has Stockfish 10, so no real reason to buy
  • For analysis of grandmaster games, Agadmator's YT channel or Kingcrusher or Mato channels are also good

I don't know much about Chessbase as I haven't used it - it is for chess players who are above 2000 fide

My chess and Chennai Chess Club

I started learning chess in Bangalore when i was quite young and I had some formal training but not much. I then represented my college team (grade 11) but didn't play much post that

I started to teach after I did my Chess Trainer Workshop at ECC. Since then I've done 3 levels of chess teacher training and also help train junior trainers.

I was part of a prestigious 4 year Dept of Science and Technology project through ECC

My FIDE rating probably doesn't reflect my level because I just don't play tournaments.

I started Chennai Chess Club to have children in my area get chess training
